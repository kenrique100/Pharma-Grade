What Is Anastrozole (Arimidex)?

Anastrozole is a third-generation non-steroidal aromatase inhibitor (AI) that blocks the conversion of testosterone into estrogen. It is commonly used during anabolic steroid cycles to maintain hormonal balance, reduce estrogenic side effects, and support lean muscle gains.

Benefits of Arimidex 2 mg

  • Prevents gynecomastia and bloating
  • Improves muscle definition by reducing water retention
  • Stabilizes estrogen during testosterone or Dianabol cycles
  • May support testosterone production indirectly post-cycle

How to Use Arimidex 2 mg

Typical on-cycle dosing is 0.5–1 mg every other day, adjusted based on estrogenic symptoms and bloodwork. Overuse can lead to excessively low estrogen, causing joint pain, low libido, and fatigue. Bloodwork is recommended for precise management.

When to Use Arimidex

It is best used during cycles that include aromatizing compounds such as:

  • Testosterone Enanthate
  • Dianabol 20 mg

Post-Cycle Therapy (PCT) Note

Arimidex is not ideal for PCT as it may suppress estrogen too strongly. Instead, use Clomid or Nolvadex after cycle completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I take Arimidex during a cycle?

Most users take 0.5–1 mg every other day during cycles with aromatizing steroids. Bloodwork helps fine-tune dosing.

Can Arimidex be used in PCT?

It's not recommended for PCT. SERMs like Clomid or Nolvadex are preferred since they stimulate natural testosterone without oversuppressing estrogen.

What are signs of low estrogen from Arimidex?

Symptoms include joint pain, mood swings, low libido, and fatigue. Reduce dosage or frequency if these appear.
