What Is Cardiogen?

Cardiogen is modeled after natural peptides involved in cardiac repair signaling, particularly those that influence angiogenesis, fibrosis modulation, and cellular regeneration within cardiac muscle. It is being studied for its therapeutic potential in cases of heart failure, ischemia-reperfusion injury, and vascular damage induced by oxidative stress or metabolic dysfunction.

Mechanism of Action

Cardiogen is believed to activate pathways related to VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) and IGF-1, enhancing oxygen delivery, reducing tissue necrosis, and improving microvascular regeneration. Its ability to modulate pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce scar tissue formation makes it a strong candidate for post-MI (myocardial infarction) research.
