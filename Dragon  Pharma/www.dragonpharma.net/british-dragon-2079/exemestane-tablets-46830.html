Frequently Asked Questions about Exemestane Tablets

How does Exemestane differ from Anastrozole?

Exemestane is a suicidal (irreversible) aromatase inhibitor, while Anastrozole is a reversible inhibitor. Exemestane permanently deactivates aromatase enzymes.

Is Exemestane better for high-dose testosterone cycles?

Yes, Exemestane is more suitable when dealing with high aromatization, such as cycles involving large doses of testosterone or Dianabol.

Should Exemestane be used during PCT?

No, Exemestane is typically used during cycle. For PCT, SERMs like Clomiphene or Tamoxifen are preferred.

What are common side effects of Exemestane?

Possible side effects include joint discomfort, lowered libido, fatigue, or cholesterol imbalance if overdosed.
