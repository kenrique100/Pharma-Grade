Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between bacteriostatic water and sterile water?

The key difference is the presence of benzyl alcohol as a preservative. Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol, which inhibits microbial growth and allows reconstituted peptides to be used multiple times over 4-6 weeks when refrigerated. Sterile water has no preservatives and is intended for single-use applications only, as it provides no protection against bacterial growth once opened. For peptide research, bacteriostatic water is generally preferred as it extends the usable life of reconstituted peptides.

How long does bacteriostatic water last after opening?

An unopened vial of bacteriostatic water typically has a shelf life of 1-2 years when stored at room temperature away from direct light. Once the rubber stopper has been punctured, the vial should be used within 28 days for optimal preservative efficacy, even if refrigerated. The benzyl alcohol concentration gradually decreases after opening, reducing its effectiveness against microbial growth over time. Always mark the date of first use on the vial.

Can I use bacteriostatic water for all types of peptides?

Bacteriostatic water is suitable for most research peptides, but there are exceptions. The majority of growth hormone releasing peptides (GHRPs), growth hormone releasing hormones (GHRHs), and healing peptides are compatible with bacteriostatic water. However, some particularly fragile peptides or those with specific molecular structures may require alternative reconstitution solutions. Always consult the specific storage requirements for each peptide compound. For peptides known to be sensitive to benzyl alcohol, sterile water without preservatives may be recommended for immediate single-use applications.

How should I store bacteriostatic water and reconstituted peptides?

Unopened bacteriostatic water should be stored at room temperature (68-77°F) away from direct light. After opening, it can be stored at room temperature or refrigerated, but should be used within 28 days. Reconstituted peptides should always be refrigerated (36-46°F) and protected from light. Avoid freezing reconstituted peptides unless specifically indicated, as freeze-thaw cycles can degrade peptide structure. Never expose peptides to excessive heat or direct sunlight, as this can rapidly degrade their molecular integrity.

Why does Peptide Hubs' bacteriostatic water contain exactly 0.9% benzyl alcohol?

This concentration is sufficient to inhibit microbial growth (bacteria, fungi, and other contaminants) while being low enough to avoid potential interactions with peptide structures. Higher concentrations could potentially affect peptide stability or cause precipitation, while lower concentrations might not provide adequate protection against microbial contamination. This 0.9% standard is consistent with USP guidelines for bacteriostatic water for injection.
